Cultural Immersion

The Tribal Trail.

The Satpura forest is home to the Gond and Korku tribes — communities who have lived within this ecosystem for centuries and carry irreplaceable knowledge of forest medicine, sustainable living, and ancestral craft.

Through formal community partnership, villa owners and guests gain access to guided cultural experiences that are deeply respectful, reciprocal, and transformative.

Our Promise

Built with the forest.
Not against it.

Sustainability is not a feature at 0 AQI Eco Retreat — it is the foundation. Every decision, from materials to operations, is governed by a non-negotiable commitment to the ecosystem.

Zero-Waste Operations
Organic waste composting, no single-use plastics, and a closed-loop waste management system that keeps the forest floor clean.
Solar Energy
Solar panels across all common areas. Grid-connected backup ensures 24/7 clean power with a measurably reduced carbon footprint.
Rainwater Harvesting
Dedicated rainwater collection systems reduce groundwater dependency. Satpura's high annual rainfall makes this a naturally effective, self-sustaining cycle.
Eco-Conscious Materials
Stone, teak, and natural local materials — no synthetic finishes. Structures are designed to age with the forest, not against it.
Women-Led Employment
Housekeeping, kitchen, and service roles are actively staffed by women from nearby villages, working through formal Self-Help Group partnerships.
Native Plant Landscaping
Only indigenous species are planted. No invasives, no artificial lawns. The landscape is designed to attract, not repel, the forest's natural inhabitants.
